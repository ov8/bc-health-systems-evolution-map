# BC Health Systems Evolution Map

An interactive historical and strategic visualization of clinical systems evolution across British Columbia healthcare organizations.

This project maps major deployment milestones, platform transitions, interoperability initiatives, and digital health modernization activities across BC’s provincial healthcare ecosystem.

The goal is to provide historical and operational context for:
- enterprise architecture
- digital transformation planning
- interoperability discussions
- governance and funding conversations
- clinical systems modernization
- long-term strategic decision-making

The visualization includes deployment and modernization activities across:
- Vancouver Coastal Health (VCH)
- Fraser Health Authority (FHA)
- Interior Health Authority (IHA)
- Northern Health Authority (NHA)
- Island Health (VIHA)
- Provincial Health Services Authority (PHSA)
- Providence Health Care (PHC)

## Purpose

This project was created to help healthcare leaders, architects, analysts, clinicians, transformation teams, and governance groups better understand:
- how BC’s clinical systems evolved over time
- where major modernization waves occurred
- historical fragmentation and convergence patterns
- interoperability dependencies
- and how historical decisions continue to shape today’s digital health landscape

## Features

- Interactive horizontal and vertical timeline views
- Health Authority swimlanes
- Expand/collapse filtering
- Facility search
- System grouping by launch event/date
- Responsive browser-based visualization
- GitHub Pages hosting support
